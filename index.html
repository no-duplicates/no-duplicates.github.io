About

I am a PhD student in Electrical & Computer Engineering at the University of Washington. My research interests are in optimization, control, and learning in multi-agent systems, especially game dynamics, minimax optimization, and stability analysis.

Research Interests

  • Optimization and game dynamics, including OGDA, GDA, and minimax problems.
  • Control and stability analysis for dynamical systems.
  • Multi-agent reinforcement learning and safety-oriented learning systems.
  • Robotic manipulation with tactile sensing and distributed control.

Selected Projects

Optimistic Gradient Dynamics and Augmented Lagrangian Methods

Studied the relationship between optimistic updates and augmented Lagrangian dynamics in constrained and minimax optimization. My focus was on stability intuition, matrix-form derivations, and presentation of the additivity structure.

Touch-Aware Robotic Manipulation with Magnetic Tactile Sensors

Capstone project on tactile-guided robotic manipulation using magnetic tactile sensors, visual input, and a distributed control architecture. I contributed to user-needs analysis, system architecture, classifier/controller design, and design-review reports.

Human Balancing on a Log: A Switched Multi-Layer Controller

Presented at ACC 2025. Work related to switched control structures for modeling human balancing behavior.
